CBT App Core Concept

App Name Idea: CBT Companion / ThoughtCare / Mind Balance CBT

Purpose:
Help users identify distressing situations, recognize automatic thoughts, evaluate those thoughts, develop balanced alternatives, and practice behavioral change through structured exercises.

This app should not behave like a “chatbot therapist” replacing a psychologist. It should work as a guided CBT self-help and therapist-support tool.

3. Thought Record Module

This is the heart of a Beck-style CBT app.

Thought Record Steps
Step 1: Situation

“What happened?”

Example:
“My manager called me suddenly.”

Step 2: Emotion

“What did you feel?”

Anxiety
Sadness
Anger
Guilt
Shame
Fear

Also ask intensity: 0–100%

Step 3: Automatic Thought

“What went through your mind?”

Example:
“I will make a mistake and they will blame me.”

Beck’s model gives major importance to automatic thoughts and how people interpret situations, not only the situation itself.

Step 4: Evidence For

“What makes this thought feel true?”

Step 5: Evidence Against

“What facts show this may not be fully true?”

Step 6: Balanced Thought

“What is a more realistic way to see this?”

Example:
“I may feel nervous, but I have handled similar work before. I can ask for clarification.”

Step 7: Re-rate Emotion

“How intense is the emotion now?”

This gives the user visible proof that thinking differently can reduce emotional intensity.

4. Cognitive Distortion Detector

The app can help the user identify thinking errors.

Common CBT Distortions
All-or-nothing thinking
Catastrophizing
Mind reading
Fortune telling
Overgeneralization
Emotional reasoning
Personalization
Should statements
Discounting positives
Labeling
Feature Example

User writes:
“I always fail in meetings.”

App detects:
Possible distortion: Overgeneralization
CBT question: “Is it true that you always fail, or are there times you managed well?”

5. Guided Discovery / Socratic Questions

CBT should not simply tell the user “think positive.” It should help the user discover a more balanced view.

CBT commonly uses guided discovery and Socratic questioning as cognitive techniques.

App Questions
What is the evidence?
Is there another explanation?
What would you tell a friend in this situation?
Are you confusing possibility with probability?
What is the worst, best, and most realistic outcome?
Will this matter after one month?
What action can you take now?

This feature can be built as a guided question flow, not a free random chatbot.

Features

Mood Before / After

Ask:

Mood before activity: 0–10
Mood after activity: 0–10

This helps users see the connection between behavior and mood.

7. Behavioral Experiments

This is powerful for anxiety, fear, low confidence, and avoidance.

Feature Flow
Step 1: Prediction

“What do you think will happen?”

Example:
“If I ask a question in a meeting, people will think I am foolish.”

Step 2: Experiment

“What small action will you try?”

Example:
“I will ask one simple clarification question.”

Step 3: Result

“What actually happened?”

Step 4: Learning

“What did you learn?”

CBT includes behavioral experiments and activity scheduling as behavioral techniques.
